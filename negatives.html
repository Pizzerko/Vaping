Symptoms From Vaping

Vaping affects the health of your throat and lungs. Although the symptoms are seemingly mild, vape slowly worsens the health of your respitory system

and produces harsher conditions and diseases. You don't want popcorn lungs or penumonia. Trust us.

2. Contains A Lot of Nicotine

There's misinformation that vaping is less dangerous, specifically contains less nicotine, than cigarettes. But, any amount of nicotine is dangerous and levels of nicotine within vape is similar to cigarettes.

But what's dangerous about nicotine?

Nicotine harms parts of the brain, especially if it's still developing, that control attention, learning, mood, and impulse control. Additionally, vape will increase the risk of getting addicted to other drugs.

Even worse, many e-cigarettes don't include that they contain nicotine, convincing many victims of indulging in these toxins.

3. IT'S ADDICTIVE!

As mentioned earlier, addiction can increase the risk to addiction and use of other drugs. Being addicted, one continue to consume more nicotine, endangering the body further long-term. Plus, nicotine doesn't affect just one's physical health, but emotionally as well.

Without the daily dose of nicotine, one may suffer from nicotine withdrawal, causing irratibility, stress for dependancy, depression, sleep troubles, anxiety, and much more. In short, nicotine ruins lives.

4. Inhaling Harmful Substances

What you inhale from vaping isn't just water vapor, nicotine, and smoke. E-cigarettes contain many micro metals and dangerous chemicals, found to lead to various diseases and cancer. Some metals include nickel, tin, and kead.

Additionally, there are flavorings such as diacetyl, a chemical found to cause serious lung damage. Even worse, some e-cigarettes are marketed as nicotine free and contain nothing about chemicals. This makes it difficult for consumers to know what they're inhaling.

5. External Dangers

Children and adults get poisoned by swallowing, breathing, or absorbing e-cigarette liquid through skin or eye contact. Nationally, 50% of calls to poison control is for e-cigar poisoning for children five years or younger.

Outside of poisoning, e-cigarettes can explode. Yes, fires have been caused, and people have suffered from serious injuries. Plus, the second hand smoke from vapers can also affect those nearby negatively, since second hand smoke is more dangerous than first hand.

Reflection
